External Resources

This page gathers a selection of reliable external resources for studying Plato and ancient Greek philosophy, from introductory material to more advanced research tools.

The links below are curated with students and independent readers in mind. They are meant to complement — not replace — direct engagement with the texts.

Reference Works

Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy

Authoritative, peer-reviewed articles on Plato, ancient philosophy, and related themes. Best starting point for serious study.

Visit →

Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy

Clear and accessible articles written by specialists. Particularly useful for students encountering Plato for the first time.

Visit →

Texts and Translations

Perseus / Scaife Viewer

Greek texts with English translations, morphological tools, and stable references. Essential for close reading.

Open →

MIT Internet Classics Archive

Public-domain English translations of Plato and other ancient authors. Useful for quick access and citation.

Open →

Loeb Classical Library

Bilingual Greek–English editions widely used in academic contexts. Subscription-based, but a gold standard.

Visit →

Courses and Structured Learning

Ancient Philosophy (Coursera)

University-level course on Plato and his predecessors. Clear structure and high-quality lectures.

View →

Open Yale Courses

Public lectures on political philosophy, including extended readings of Plato’s Republic.

Visit →

Video Resources

Wireless Philosophy

Short, well-designed videos explaining key philosophical problems, including several drawn from Plato’s dialogues.

Watch →

Podcasts and Audio

History of Philosophy Without Any Gaps

A comprehensive podcast covering the history of philosophy from the Presocratics onward, with extensive episodes on Plato.

Listen →

BBC In Our Time

Accessible discussions with scholars on classical topics, including Plato, Socrates, and ancient Athens.
