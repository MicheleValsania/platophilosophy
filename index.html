Learning through Dialogue

Plato wrote philosophy as dialogue: ideas take shape through questions, not lectures.

You: What is justice?

Plato: Before we answer, let us ask what we usually mean when we use this word.

When we call someone “just”, do we mean someone who follows the law — or someone who acts according to what is right?

The dialogue adapts to your level and helps you explore ideas through questions.
