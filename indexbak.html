Learning through Dialogue

Plato wrote philosophy as dialogue.

Questions lead to other questions. Definitions are tested, revised, and sometimes abandoned. The dialogue experience is designed to accompany the reader — not to judge or evaluate — but to help thinking take shape.

You: What is justice?

Plato: That is an important question. Before we try to answer it, let us ask what we usually mean when we use this word.

Tell me — when we speak of a just person, do we mean someone who follows the law, or someone who acts according to what is right?

The dialogue adapts to your level and helps you explore ideas through questions, not lectures.
