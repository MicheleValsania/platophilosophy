Timeline

A chronological overview of key events in Plato's life and the historical context surrounding them.

428/427 BC
Plato born in Athens to an aristocratic family. Father: Ariston. Mother: Perictione (related to Solon).
431 BC
Peloponnesian War begins (Athens vs. Sparta). Plato grows up during wartime.
c. 408 BC
Plato meets Socrates at age 20. Devotes himself to philosophy, abandons plans for politics or poetry.
404 BC
Athens defeated by Sparta. Thirty Tyrants briefly rule Athens (including Plato's relatives Critias and Charmides).
403 BC
Democracy restored in Athens. Political tensions remain.
399 BC
Socrates tried and executed on charges of impiety and corrupting youth. Plato present at trial, absent at death. Formative trauma.
399-387 BC
Plato's travels: Megara (with Eucleides), possibly Egypt, Cyrene (Theodorus), Italy (Archytas and Pythagoreans), Sicily (meets Dion, fails with Dionysius I).
387 BC
Plato returns to Athens and founds the Academy — first institution of higher learning in the Western world.
367 BC
Second visit to Syracuse. Attempts to educate Dionysius II. Dion exiled. Plato returns frustrated.
367-347 BC
Aristotle joins Academy at age 17, studies there for 20 years until Plato's death.
361 BC
Third visit to Syracuse. Essentially held hostage. Rescued by Archytas. Final political failure.
360-348 BC
Plato's final years. Continues teaching at Academy. Writes late dialogues: Timaeus, Laws (unfinished).
348/347 BC
Plato dies in Athens at age 80-81. Buried at Academy. Speusippus becomes second scholarch.
529 AD
Academy closed by Byzantine emperor Justinian after nearly 1,000 years of continuous operation.
