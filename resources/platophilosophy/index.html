Plato's Life

Understanding Plato's life helps illuminate his philosophy. Born into an aristocratic Athenian family during a time of war and political upheaval, he witnessed the trial and death of Socrates, traveled across the Mediterranean, and founded the Academy — one of the first institutions of higher learning in the Western world.

This section guides you through the key moments and contexts of Plato's life, from his birth in 428 BC to his death around 348 BC.

Timeline Overview

Plato lived for approximately 80 years, witnessing dramatic changes in Athens and Greece. His life can be divided into several key periods.
