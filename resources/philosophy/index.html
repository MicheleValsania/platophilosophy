Plato's Philosophy

Plato's philosophical system encompasses metaphysics, epistemology, ethics, and political theory. From the Theory of Forms to the structure of the ideal state, his ideas have shaped Western thought for over two millennia.

This section explores Plato's central concepts: what is real, how we know, how we should live, and how society should be organized. Each page examines a key idea with references to the dialogues where Plato developed these arguments.
